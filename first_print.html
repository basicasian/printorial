> Chapter 3: Preparing Your Printer for the Print

Before you can start your print, it‚Äôs important to ensure your Bambu Lab A1 Mini is properly prepared. This includes basic setup, maintenance checks, and confirming the connection between your slicer (Bambu Studio) and the printer.

  1. Power on and update firmware:
    Make sure the printer is plugged in and turned on. Check for any available firmware updates via the touchscreen interface.
  2. Load filamentFilament is the thermoplastic material used by 3D printers to create objects layer by layer. :
    Use the automatic filament loading system. Insert the filament into the AMS unit The AMS Unit is an automatic system that holds and feeds multiple filament spools to a 3D printer, allowing longer or multi-color prints without manual changes. . (if available) or directly into the extruderAn extruder is the part of a 3D printer that melts and pushes the filament through a nozzle to build the object layer by layer. . You need to push it until it stops. Then go to the screen and click on to the fillament button and then on load. If the printer asks you for additional information on your fillament click yes. Select 'Bambu Lab' and 'PLA Basic' and click OK. Than the printer will go through some automatic steps. Afterwards a new prompt will pop up asking you if you see the fillament coming out of the extruder. Check this and click DONE if fine.
  3. Check Wi-Fi or USB connection:
    Ensure the printer is connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your computer for seamless slicing and remote printing. Alternatively, use a microSD card or USB drive to transfer the sliced file.
  4. Preheat if necessary:
    While Bambu Studio will typically handle this, you can manually preheat the nozzle and bed if needed via the touchscreen.
  5. Double-check materials and bed:
    Confirm that:
    • The filament is loaded and extruding correctly
    • The print bed is clean and free of debris
    • You‚Äôre using the correct build plate for your material type

  6. Once your printer is ready and connected, you can move on to sending the file from Bambu Studio and starting the print! You have two options from the dropdown: You either select 'print', which will directly start the print or you can select 'send', which will send your file to the printer. When you click send you will see that the model is being downloaded to the printer. Afterwards, you can go to the printers display, select your model from there and start the print.

> Chapter 4: Printing & Refining
  • If you directly started your print: The printing process will automatically start.
  • If you send your model to the printer: Go to the printers display, click on 'print files' and select your model. Click 'next' and it will start printing your file.
  • After the print: Your model is ready! If you want to make some refinements, you can carefully sand the surface of your model.
